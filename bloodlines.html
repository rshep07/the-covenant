The Bloodlines

Five bloodlines. Each carries its own power, its own curse, its own rarity. Choose wisely.

Crimson
He gave them power over flesh. The kind that lets you take a life, keep one going, or do things to a body that no prayer, charm, or locked door can protect against. He marks it all down. Every death. Every life spared. All of it his. What they lose is harder to see. The hesitation. The mercy. The part of them that used to feel bad about it.

The thing about power over flesh is that it starts small, and it starts quiet, and it starts with something that doesn't feel like power at all. It feels like confidence. It feels like the room adjusting around you when you walk in, giving you a little more space than you need, the way people give space to a big dog even when it's not doing anything. Then one afternoon you want something, and the universe decides to provide it, and the way it provides it isn't clean, and you should feel worse about that than you do. He marks it all down. Every use, every life taken, every life kept breathing. His name is on all of it, which means his name is on you, and that was the deal. What you lose doesn't announce itself going. The hesitation is first. Then the mercy. Then the part of you that used to sit with what you'd done in the quiet afterward. You don't notice that one leaving until years later, reaching for it the way you'd reach for a tool you've misplaced, and the shelf is bare.

Mystic
The Mystic bloodline wanted what was locked away on purpose. Not rumors. Not secrets passed around by frightened people. The stuff that was hidden because knowing it changes you in ways you cannot come back from. He gave them the key, and what he gets back is everything they find. He knows what they know before they have time to understand what it means.

Here's the thing nobody tells you about knowledge: ignorance isn't just comfortable. It's load-bearing. The things people don't know are holding up the things they think they know, and when you start pulling at the hidden ones, the visible ones start to shift. The Mystic bloodline is for the ones who couldn't stop pulling. He sees everything that moves between worlds. He gave them a key to the rooms that key was never meant to open, and what he gets in return is first look at everything they find. He knows what they know before they've had time to understand what it means. The cost doesn't announce itself. One morning you start seeing things that aren't there. Then you realize they are. The life you had before you started looking is still out there somewhere, like a house you grew up in that still stands but isn't yours anymore. You can drive past it. You just can't go inside.

Spirit
He governs the dead, but the dead do not stay neatly where they are put. They wander. They cling. They get lost in doorways, bedrooms, mirrors, and places where grief has worn a path into the floor. So he made a deal. The price is belonging. They live between two worlds and are claimed by neither. The living feel something wrong before they can name it. The dead know exactly what they are.

Here's something nobody tells you about the dead: most of them are confused. Not scary. Not dangerous. Just stuck. They died in the middle of something, or they don't know they're dead, or they know and haven't figured out what to do about it yet, and so they wander. Doorways. Bedrooms. The kind of room where someone grieved for a long time, where the pacing wore a groove in the floor that you can still feel even after the carpet's been replaced. He runs the dead but he's only got the one place to be. So he made a deal. The Spirit bloodline gets to move between the living and the dead the way the rest of us move between rooms. The price is belonging. They live in two worlds and own a piece of neither. Living people feel something wrong about them before they can name it, the way you feel watched in a room you know is empty. The dead know exactly what they are.

Wild
The Wild bloodline wanted to stop being human. Not to die. To be done with the weakness of being one person in one body for one short life. He rules what the ground receives and what it gives back. He let them become part of that cycle. It starts in the skin and works inward. Quietly. Most do not notice what is missing until they reach for it and find nothing there.

You ever stand at the edge of something genuinely large and feel the pull? The ocean at night, or a canyon, or one of those old-growth forests where the trees are wider around than a car and the ground smells like something that's been doing the same job for ten thousand years. Not the dark kind of pull, not the falling kind. The other one. The one that says: you could stop being so much yourself for a while. Most people step back from that. The Wild bloodline is for the ones who leaned in. They wanted to be done with the exhaustion of one short life in a world that doesn't particularly notice you're there. He rules what goes into the ground and what comes back up. He let them in. The deal starts in the skin and works inward, quiet and thorough, replacing what's human with whatever was living there before humans came along. Some of them are genuinely relieved. Most are fine with it, right up until they reach for something familiar and nothing's there.

Cursed
The Cursed bloodline never made a deal. Someone before them did. The terms were broken. The debt went unpaid. He does not forgive debts. He passes them down. They were born already owing. He could end it. He just has not decided they are worth the answer.

My grandfather used to say every family has a skeleton in the closet. He meant it as a figure of speech. A secret. Some old shame getting passed down because nobody wants to be the one to drag it out into the light. The Cursed bloodline is literal about it. Somewhere back in the tree, somebody made a deal they couldn't keep. They took what he was offering and then didn't hold up their end, and he doesn't do forgiveness. What he does is inheritance. The debt passes down, quiet as bad eyes or a weak heart, until there's someone alive to collect from. Every other bloodline at least chose. Every one of them walked to that crossroads wanting something badly enough to pay for it. The Cursed came into the world already on the hook. He knows the original deal down to the handshake. He could close it out tomorrow. He hasn't decided they're worth the trouble yet.
